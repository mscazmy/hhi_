Department of Nursing

Overview

The Department of Nursing is the founding and largest department of HHI Wasit. Established to address the critical shortage of qualified nurses in Wasit Governorate, the program offers a comprehensive four-year bachelor's degree curriculum covering biological sciences, clinical nursing skills, pharmacology, community health, and nursing management.

Graduates are equipped to work in hospitals, primary health care centers, schools, and community settings across Iraq. The department maintains strong clinical partnerships with Al-Kut Teaching Hospital and several district health centers.

Program Objectives

  • Develop comprehensive patient assessment and care planning skills
  • Apply evidence-based nursing practice in clinical settings
  • Build proficiency in medication administration and safety protocols
  • Foster professional communication and teamwork with healthcare teams
  • Prepare graduates for leadership roles in nursing and healthcare management

Curriculum Highlights

Year 1 & 2

  • Human Anatomy & Physiology
  • Fundamentals of Nursing
  • Medical Microbiology
  • Pharmacology I & II
  • Nutrition & Dietetics

Year 3 & 4

  • Medical-Surgical Nursing
  • Pediatric & Neonatal Nursing
  • Mental Health Nursing
  • Community & Public Health Nursing
  • Research Methods & Thesis

Department of Emergency Medicine

Overview

The Department of Emergency Medicine prepares students to respond rapidly and effectively to acute medical emergencies in hospital and pre-hospital environments. The curriculum integrates emergency nursing skills, advanced life support, disaster preparedness, and trauma care, meeting national and international standards for emergency health practitioners.

Students benefit from state-of-the-art simulation labs featuring high-fidelity manikins, allowing them to practice resuscitation, intubation, wound management, and mass casualty triage in a safe environment before clinical placement.

Core Competencies

Advanced Life Support

CPR, defibrillation, airway management, and resuscitation protocols

Trauma Care

Hemorrhage control, fracture immobilization, burn management, shock assessment

Triage & Disaster Response

START/SALT triage, mass casualty incident management, hospital surge protocols

Quick Facts

Department of Midwifery

Overview

The Midwifery Department trains skilled birth attendants and reproductive health specialists who play a vital role in reducing maternal and neonatal mortality in Wasit and surrounding regions. The four-year program covers anatomy and physiology of reproduction, obstetric nursing, neonatal care, family planning, and women's health across the life cycle.

Clinical placements at Wasit Maternity Hospital allow students to attend deliveries, manage routine and complicated pregnancies, and provide postnatal care under expert supervision. The department has been nationally accredited and is working toward regional recognition.

Program Structure

Antepartum Care

  • Antenatal assessment & counseling
  • High-risk pregnancy management
  • Nutrition in pregnancy
  • Fetal monitoring techniques

Intrapartum & Postpartum

  • Normal and assisted deliveries
  • Management of obstetric emergencies
  • Newborn resuscitation
  • Postnatal care & breastfeeding support

Neonatal Care

  • Neonatal assessment (APGAR)
  • Care of the low-birthweight infant
  • Neonatal infections
  • Kangaroo mother care

Women's Health

  • Family planning methods
  • Reproductive health education
  • Gynecological nursing
  • Health promotion & advocacy
